# Florida Cities Overview

This document provides overview information for major Florida cities to be ingested into the MyCarMindATO agent data system.

## Jacksonville, Florida

**Overview**: Jacksonville is Florida's most populous city and the largest city by area in the contiguous United States. Located in northeast Florida along the Atlantic coast and St. Johns River, it's known for its extensive park system, beaches, and military presence.

**Key Facts**:
- Largest city in Florida by population
- 22 miles of beaches including Jacksonville Beach, Neptune Beach, and Atlantic Beach
- Major port city with a strong military presence (Naval Station Mayport, NAS Jacksonville)
- Growing arts and culture scene in downtown

**Things to Do**:
- Visit the Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens
- Explore the historic Riverside and Avondale neighborhoods
- Relax at Jacksonville Beach and the beach towns
- See wildlife at Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens
- Stroll through Riverside Arts Market

**Local Businesses**: River City Brewing Company, Bold City Brewery, Intuition Ale Works, Black Sheep Restaurant, Orsay

**Parks & Outdoors**: Kathryn Abbey Hanna Park, Big Talbot Island State Park, Fort Clinch State Park, Timucuan Ecological & Historic Preserve

**Neighborhoods**: Riverside, Avondale, San Marco, Springfield, Jacksonville Beach

**Comparisons/Highlights**: Jacksonville is often described as a sprawling Southern city with beach access - less touristy than Miami or Orlando, but with a growing urban core and natural beauty.

## Miami, Florida

**Overview**: Miami is a vibrant, multicultural metropolis in southeast Florida, known for its beaches, Art Deco architecture, Latin American influence, and nightlife. It's a major international hub for finance, commerce, culture, and entertainment.

**Key Facts**:
- Gateway to Latin America with diverse international population
- Famous for South Beach, Art Deco Historic District
- Year-round warm tropical climate
- Major cruise port and international business center
- Bilingual city (English and Spanish widely spoken)

**Things to Do**:
- Experience South Beach and Ocean Drive
- Explore Wynwood Walls street art district
- Visit Vizcaya Museum and Gardens
- Tour Little Havana and try Cuban coffee
- Shop and dine in Miami Design District
- Visit the Pérez Art Museum Miami (PAMM)

**Local Businesses**: Versailles Restaurant (Cuban), Joe's Stone Crab, Ball & Chain (Little Havana), Zak the Baker, Mister O1 Pizza

**Parks & Outdoors**: South Pointe Park, Bayfront Park, Oleta River State Park, Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park, Everglades National Park (nearby)

**Neighborhoods**: South Beach, Wynwood, Little Havana, Coconut Grove, Brickell, Coral Gables, Miami Beach

**Comparisons/Highlights**: Miami is the Latin American capital of the U.S., combining Caribbean flair with cosmopolitan sophistication - think beaches, art, nightlife, and international culture in one vibrant package.

## Tampa, Florida

**Overview**: Tampa is a major city on Florida's Gulf Coast, known for its historic Ybor City neighborhood, waterfront, museums, and family attractions. It's part of the Tampa Bay metropolitan area and offers a mix of urban amenities and outdoor recreation.

**Key Facts**:
- Located on Tampa Bay with a revitalized downtown waterfront (Riverwalk)
- Historic Ybor City known for cigar-making heritage
- Home to Busch Gardens theme park
- Strong Cuban and Spanish cultural influences
- Major port and economic center

**Things to Do**:
- Walk the Tampa Riverwalk
- Explore historic Ybor City's brick streets
- Visit Busch Gardens Tampa Bay
- Tour the Florida Aquarium
- See exhibits at Tampa Museum of Art
- Catch a Lightning game (NHL) or Buccaneers game (NFL)

**Local Businesses**: Columbia Restaurant (Ybor City, oldest restaurant in Florida), Cigar City Brewing, Ulele, Oxford Exchange, Tampa Bay Brewing Company

**Parks & Outdoors**: Curtis Hixon Waterfront Park, Lettuce Lake Park, Hillsborough River State Park, nearby beaches (Clearwater, St. Pete)

**Neighborhoods**: Ybor City, Hyde Park, Seminole Heights, Downtown Tampa, Channelside

**Comparisons/Highlights**: Tampa combines working-class authenticity with cultural attractions - it's less flashy than Miami but offers history, great food (Cuban sandwiches!), and proximity to world-class beaches.

## Orlando, Florida

**Overview**: Orlando is Central Florida's theme park capital and one of the world's most visited tourist destinations. Beyond the parks, it has a growing downtown, diverse neighborhoods, and numerous lakes.

**Key Facts**:
- Home to Walt Disney World, Universal Orlando, SeaWorld
- More than 100 lakes within city limits
- Rapidly growing tech and healthcare industries
- No beaches in Orlando proper (closest is about an hour away)
- Subtropical climate with hot, humid summers

**Things to Do**:
- Visit Walt Disney World Resort
- Experience Universal Orlando Resort (including Wizarding World of Harry Potter)
- Explore Downtown Orlando and Lake Eola Park
- Visit Kennedy Space Center (nearby on Space Coast)
- Shop at Disney Springs or Universal CityWalk
- Tour the Orlando Science Center

**Local Businesses**: The Ravenous Pig, Se7en Bites, Prato, 4 Rivers Smokehouse, Gatorland

**Parks & Outdoors**: Lake Eola Park, Harry P. Leu Gardens, Wekiwa Springs State Park, Tibet-Butler Nature Preserve

**Neighborhoods**: Winter Park, Thornton Park, College Park, Baldwin Park, Mills 50

**Comparisons/Highlights**: Orlando is synonymous with theme parks and family vacation magic, but it's also a real city with local culture, dining scenes, and natural springs - the ultimate blend of fantasy and reality.

## St. Petersburg, Florida

**Overview**: St. Petersburg (often called St. Pete) is a waterfront city on Florida's Gulf Coast, known for its arts scene, beautiful downtown, craft breweries, and pristine beaches. It's across Tampa Bay from Tampa.

**Key Facts**:
- Consistently ranked as one of America's best places to live
- Vibrant downtown with museums, galleries, and restaurants
- Home to Salvador Dalí Museum
- Average 361 days of sunshine per year
- Beautiful beaches including St. Pete Beach and Treasure Island

**Things to Do**:
- Visit the Salvador Dalí Museum
- Stroll through The Pier district
- Explore Museum of Fine Arts St. Petersburg
- Relax on St. Pete Beach or Pass-a-Grille Beach
- Walk or bike The Pinellas Trail
- Tour Sunken Gardens
- Catch a Rays game (MLB)

**Local Businesses**: Green Bench Brewing, Cycle Brewing, Engine No. 9, The Hangar Restaurant & Flight Lounge, Red Mesa Cantina

**Parks & Outdoors**: Fort De Soto Park, Weedon Island Preserve, Vinoy Park, St. Pete Beach, Caladesi Island State Park

**Neighborhoods**: Downtown St. Petersburg, Grand Central District, Historic Old Northeast, St. Pete Beach

**Comparisons/Highlights**: St. Pete is the artsy, laid-back alternative to Tampa - with world-class museums, a thriving craft beer scene, stunning sunsets, and accessible beaches, it's Florida's cultural gem on the Gulf.
